# AI Reports

AI Reports generate on-demand diagnostic summaries of your infrastructure. Choose from a set of targeted prompts, and the AI produces a structured analysis — root causes, affected resources, and recommended actions — based on your actual environment state.

## FAQs

<details>
<summary>
### What is an AI Report?
</summary>
An AI Report is a structured analysis of your infrastructure generated on demand. You select a prompt — for example, "Why is this service responding slowly?" or "What changed in the last deployment?" — and the AI produces a clear breakdown of root causes, the resources involved, and the actions you should take.

Reports are different from Autodiagnostics: Autodiagnostics targets a single resource in response to an error, while Reports let you ask broader questions across multiple resources or time windows.
</details>

<details>
<summary>
### Where do I find AI Reports?
</summary>
AI Reports are available from the **Reports** section in the SleakOps sidebar. You can also generate a report directly from a resource's detail view when a report prompt is available for that context.
</details>

<details>
<summary>
### What prompts are available?
</summary>
The Reports catalog includes prompts for common operational questions:

- Service health and performance degradation
- Deployment history and configuration drift
- Dependency connectivity and availability
- Resource utilization and cost anomalies

The catalog grows with each release. If a prompt you need is missing, [Infrastructure Chat](conversation) lets you ask ad-hoc questions.
</details>

<details>
<summary>
### How is a Report different from Autodiagnostics?
</summary>

| | Autodiagnostics | AI Reports |
|---|---|---|
| Trigger | On resource failure | Manual, on demand |
| Scope | Single resource | Resource, environment, or cluster |
| Focus | "What broke and why?" | "What is the state of X across Y?" |
| Output | Root cause + fix suggestion | Structured analysis with context |

Use Autodiagnostics when something is already broken. Use Reports to proactively understand the state of your infrastructure.
</details>

<details>
<summary>
### Are reports saved?
</summary>
Yes. Generated reports are saved in the SleakOps console and can be revisited, shared with teammates, or used as context when opening a support ticket.
</details>
