# AI Reports

AI Reports generate on-demand diagnostic summaries of your infrastructure. Click **Create Report**, select a pre-defined AI prompt, and get a structured analysis — root causes, affected resources, and recommended actions — based on your actual environment state.

## FAQs

<details>
<summary>
### Where do I find AI Reports?
</summary>
AI Reports live inside **Settings → Reports** (`/reports/`). Navigate to Settings from the sidebar and select **Reports** from the left menu.
</details>

<details>
<summary>
### How do I generate a report?
</summary>
1. Navigate to **Settings → Reports**.
2. Click **+ Create Report**.
3. A panel opens showing **AI Assistant Prompts** — a searchable catalog of pre-defined analysis prompts.

<Zoom overlayBgColorEnd="rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.8)">
  <img
    src={require("./reports/reports-ai-prompts.png").default}
    alt="ai-assistant-prompts-panel"
  />
</Zoom>

4. Select a prompt from the catalog or type in the message field to ask a custom question.
5. The AI generates a structured report based on your live infrastructure state.
</details>

<details>
<summary>
### What prompts are available?
</summary>
The Reports catalog includes pre-defined prompts for common operational questions such as service health, deployment history, dependency availability, and resource utilization. Use the search box in the **AI Assistant Prompts** panel to find the prompt that matches your situation.

You can also type a free-form question directly in the message field at the bottom of the panel.
</details>

<details>
<summary>
### How is a Report different from Autodiagnostics?
</summary>

| | Autodiagnostics | AI Reports |
|---|---|---|
| Trigger | Activated from a specific resource with an issue | Manual, on demand from Settings → Reports |
| Scope | Single resource | Configurable — resource, environment, or cluster |
| Focus | "What broke and why?" | "What is the state of X across Y?" |
| Output | Root cause + fix suggestion | Structured analysis with context |

Use Autodiagnostics when something is already broken. Use Reports to proactively understand the state of your infrastructure.
</details>

<details>
<summary>
### Are reports saved?
</summary>
Yes. Generated reports are saved in the Reports list and can be revisited at any time from **Settings → Reports**.
</details>
